And after six days Yeshua took Kepha, and Ya'aqov, and Yochanan his brother, and took them up upon a high mountain by themselves. And his face was transformed before them, and his face shone like [the] sun. And his clothes were white like snow. And behold, Moshe and EliYahu appeared to them, speaking with him {and telling Yeshua everything that would meet him in Yerushalayim. And Peter and his companions were sleeping. Asleep but not asleep. Awake but not awake}. And Kepha answered, and he said to Yeshua, "Adonai, it's good for us to be here. If you want, then we will make three tents {dwellings}: one for you, one for Moshe, one for EliYahu {Eloah}" {since he did not know what he was saying}. They were yet talking, and behold, a splendorous cloud covered them. And behold, a voice spoke from the cloud: "This one, he is my beloved Son who I have been pleased with. {My precious Son, and my delight [is] in him}. Listen to him." And his taught ones heard, and fell on their faces, and feared exceedingly. And Yeshua drew near, and touched them, and said to them, "rise up, and do not fear." And they lifted up their eyes, and saw not but Yeshua alone. And as they were coming down from the mountain, Yeshua commanded them, saying, "don't speak to any man of the sight that ye have seen until the Son of man has risen from the dead." And his taught-ones asked him, saying, "why do the scribes say that EliYahu must come first?" And he answered, and said to them, "Yes, EliYahu will come, and he will restore all {and he will save the whole world}, and I say to ye that EliYahu already came, and they did not know him, but have done to him all that they chose. And so [shall] the Son of man receive, and shall bear their torments." Then the taught-ones heard and understood that he was speaking with them for the sake of The Immerser Yochanan. And when he came to the crowd, one man drew near to him and bowed down before him upon his knees, saying, "Adonai, have tender mercy upon my son, for he is fallen, and his sicknesses much afflict him, for many times he falls in fire, many times he falls in water. And I brought him near to your taught-ones, and they were not able to heal him." And Yeshua answered, and said, "rebellious and stubborn generation, how long will I be with you, and how long will I bear ye? Bring him to me." And Yeshua rebuked him, and the demon came out of him, and the young man was healed in that hour. Then his taught-ones drew near to him in secret, and they asked him, "why weren't we able to drive him out?" And he answered them, and he said, "because ye arn't faithful. Confirmed [is what] I say to ye, if ye have faith like a mustard seed, then you will say to this mountain, leave here, and right away it would leave, and the matter would not be hindering ye. And this, this type will not be cast [out] except by prayer and fasting." And they walked to Galil, Yeshua spoke to them, and he said, "[The] Son of man will be bound by men's hands and they shall kill him, and on the third day he shall rise." And they were exceedingly pained, and when they came to Kephar Nachum, those who took drachma[-tribute] came near to Kepha, and they said to him, "Will ye[all]'s teacher pay drachma[-tribute]?" And he said, "[He] also [will] pay." And when he came into the house before Yeshua, saying, "How does it appear [that] I will go, Shimon? Kings of the earth, from who do they take tax and offering? From sons of from strangers?" And he said, "from the strangers." And Yeshua said to him, "If so, the sons are free, but so we do not anger them, go to the sea to cast the net, and take the fish that first comes up, and open its mouth, and you will find a stater [four drachma coin], and give it to them for me and for you."
