On that day Yeshua went forth from the house, and sat on [the] lip of the sea, and great crowds gathered to him, and [so] he went up into a boat, and sat down, and all the people stood upon [the] lip of the sea, and he spoke to them (the multitude) in parables, and he said: "behold, the sower went forth to sow his seed, and [it came to pass] that a portion of [the] seed[s] fell upon the road, so birds from the sky came and ate them. And others fell upon the rock where there [was] not much soil, and straightaway they grew because there wasn't deep soil for them [i.e. the morning dew on the rock's surface quickly germinated them and then evaporated], and after that, the sun raised its sword, and because they had no roots, therefore they withered. And others fell among thorns and the thorns grew bigger, and they choked them. And others fell into good soil, and they put gave forth fruits: one, one hundred, and one, sixty, and one, thirty. Whosoever has ears to hear, he will hear. And his taught-ones drew near to him, and they said to him, "my lord, why do you speak with us in parables?" And he answered, and said to them, "for He has given ye to understand secret things [of the] Kingdom of Heaven, and to these He has not given, for whosoever has, [it] will be given, and he shall abound [therein], and whomsoever has not, what he has will also be taken from him. Therefore, I speak to ye in parables that those who see, alas, they see not, and those who hear, alas, they hear not, and do not discern, to establish [the] prophecy of Isaiah in them, which says, "hearing they shall hear, and not discern, and seeing they shall see, and not know. Oil the heart of this nation, and make its ears heavy, and smear over its eyes, lest it see with its eyes, and with its ears hear, and its heart discern, and turn, and I heal it." And happy are your eyes, for they see, and your ears, for they hear. Confirmed [is what] I am saying to ye, that many prophets and righteous-ones would have been delighted to see what ye see, and did not see [it], and to hear what ye hear, and have not heard [it]. Therefore hear ye [the] parable of the sower: all who hear the word of the Kingdom, and don't understand, evil comes, and tramples what is sown in his heart. And this is he who sows on the road. And what was sown upon the rock, this is hear who hears and at once lifts himself up in joy, and is similar to a seed that has no root, and will not stand, but rather in the hour when difficulties or persecution smack, immediately he falls.
