And it came pass after Yeshua finished speaking these words, that he went from the Galil, and he came to [the] borders of Yehudah in transversing the Yordan, and great crowds walked behind him, and he healed them there. And the Prushim drew near to him, and they acted covertly, saying, "Is it permitted for man to send away his wife for any matter?" And he turned, and said to them, "have ye not read that he who made the man from the Beginning, made them male and female, and He said therefore, 'man shall leave his father and his mother and shall cleave unto his wife, and they shall become one flesh.' And now they are not two, but rather one flesh only. Therefore, that which Elohim joined, man is not able to to separate." And they said, "Why did Moshe command to give a bill of divorcement, and to send her [away] if she is not upright in his eyes?" And he answered them, and he said, "because Moshe, for the hardness of your hearts, allowed ye to send away your wives, but from the Beginning, it was not so. And I say to ye, that every man that sent away or will send away his wife, except for the matter of whoredom, and takes another, commits adultery. And whosoever takes the one driven away, he also commits adultery." And his taught-ones said to him, "if the matter is so with a wife, then it's not good to marry." And he said to them, "Not everyone may do this thing, except them to whom it was given. For there are eunuchs that from their mothers' wombs were born so. And there are eunuchs that were made by man, and there are eunuchs that made themselves eunuchs for the sake of the Kingdom of Heaven. Whosoever is able to take it, let him take it. Then young ones were brought to him, that he might put hands on them and pray. And his taught-ones drove them away and Yeshua said to them, "Allow young ones, and do not hinder them from coming to me, for these [is the] Kingdom of Heaven. And when he had laid hands upon them, he went from there, and behold, one drew near, and he said to him, "Good Rabbi, and what good [thing] shall I do so that I may purchase life of the world to come?" And he said to him, "why do you ask me concerning goodness. There is none good, except one that is good, and he is the El. And if you delight in entering into life of the world to come, gaurd the commandments of the El." And he said to him, "...and which [ones]?" And Yeshua responded, and he said, "Do not murder, do not commit adultery, do not steal, do not answer against your neighbor [as] a false witness, honor your father and your mother, and love your neighbor as yourself." And the young man said to him, "all these have I guarded since my youth, and what yet lacks me?" And Yeshua said to him, "if you want to be perfect, then sell all that you have and give to the poor, and there will be treasure in heaven for you. And come, walk behind me." And the young man heard the word, and went [away] in pain, for he had him a great inheritance. And Yeshua said to his taught-ones, "Amen I say to ye, that in difficulties the rich will enter [the] Kingdom of Heaven. And moreover I say to ye, it [is] easier to pass a camel through the hole of a needle, than for the rich to enter unto the Kingdom of Heaven." And when the taught-ones heard these words, they were very confused, saying, "And who is able to be saved?" And Yeshua turned to them, and he said, "with men this [is] not possible, but for Elohim all these [are] possible things." Then Kepha answered, and he said to him, "Behold, he have left everything, and we walk after thee, and what will be for us?" And Yeshua said to them, "Amen I say to ye, that ye that walk after me, in the second birth, when the Son of man sits on [the] throne of his glory, ye shall also sit upon twelve thrones, and ye shall judge the twelve tribes of Israel. And everyone that leaves house, or brother or sisters or father or mother, or wife or sons or field, he shall recieve one-hundred fold, and he will inherit eternal life. And many who are first will be last, and those who are last will be first.
