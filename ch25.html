Then the Kingdom of Heaven is like unto to twelve virgins that took their cresset torches, and went forth to meet the bridegroom. Five of them were foolish and five of them were wise. The foolish ones, when they took their cresset torches, did not take oil with them. And the wise ones all took oil with them with the cresset torches, and when the bridegroom was late, they all napped and slumbered. And in the middle of the night, there was a cry, and behold, the bridegroom came, and they went forth to meet him, as did all these virgins, and preparing their cresset torches. And the foolish ones said to the wise ones, 'give us of yoThereur oil' for our cresset torches have been extinguished. And the wise ones answered, and said to them, 'It is not given to ye, lest we lack.' And ye, go to them who sell to procure, and at that, for yourselves. And in their going to procure, the bridegroom came. Those prepared came with him to the wedding, and the entrance was closed, and afterwards the remnant of the other virgins came, saying, 'lord, lord, open to us', and he answered, saying, 'Confirmed [is] what I am saying to ye, that I have not known ye.' And therefore be wakeful, for ye do no know the day or the hour given that he, the Son of man, will come. For [it is] as a man that goes to sojourn in a province of the sea, and calls to his servant and gives them his possessions. To one he gave five kekars, and to one, two, and to one, one; to every man gave he according to his power, and he departed right away. And [the one] who took the five kekars went and traded with them, and he procured five others. And [the one] who took procured another two more, and [the one] who took the one, went, and dug in earth, and hid it in earth. And it was after much time, his lord returned, and he made an account with them. And the one who took the five kekars drew close, and he brought near yet five other kekars, saying, 'lord, you gave me five kekars, and behold I've added to them five others more.' And his lord said to him, 'Hurray! Because of being a good servant, and faithful in the little, come, and I will appoint you over the much. Go, enter into the joy of your lord. And he also drew near who took the two kekars, and he said, 'my lord, you have given me two kekars, and behold yet these two more I have procured.' And his lord said to him, 'Hurray! Since you were a good servant, and faithful over the little, please, and I will appoint you over the much. Go, enter into the joy of your lord.' And he drew near who took the one kekar, and he said, 'my lord, I know that you are a harsh man, and you reap where you did not sow, and you gather where you did not scatter. And I saw, and I went, and I hid your kekar in earth. Behold, what is yours [is] yours.' And his lord answered him, and he said to him, 'bad and lazy servant, you knew that I reap that where I did not sow, and I gather that where I did not scatter. You should have given your silver to the moneychangers, and so I would have been, and taken that which is mine with increase. Therefore please take the kekar and give it to he to whom [had] the ten kekars. Because for all who have, he will give to him, and he shall abound, and whosoever has nought, all what appears to have will be taken from him. And the failed servant cast into outer darkness, where there will be weeping and teeth gnashing. And when the Son of man comes in his glory, and all his Angels, then he shall sit upon the throne of his glory, and all people groups will be gathered before him, and he will separate them, these from these, as the shepard seperates the lambs from the kids, and he will set the lambs on his right and the kids to his left. And then he will say, the king, to these that will be on his right, 'go, blessed of my father, and possess the Kingdom prepared for ye from the beginning of the world. I was starving, and ye gave me [something] to eat. I was thirsty, and ye gave me [something] to drink. I was a sojourner, and you gathered me in. I was naked and you covered me. I was sick, and you visited me. I was in a house of prisoners, and you came to me. Then the righteous answered him, saying, 'my lord, when did we see you starving, and we fed you, or thirsty, and we gave you drink. And when did we see you a sojourner, and we gathered you in, or blind and we covered you, or when did we see you sick or in a house of prisoners, and we came to you? And the King will answer, and say to them, Confirmed [is] what I say to ye, that as I have done to one of these, which are to me as little brothers, likewise have ye done to me.' And then the king said to these that were on his left, 'turn from me, cursed ones, go to His eternal fire, that is prepared for Satan and his angels. For I was starving, and ye did not give me bread. I thirsted, and ye did not give me drink. I was a sojourner, and ye did not gather me in. I was naked, and you did not cover me. I was sick, and in a house of prisoners, and you did not visit me. Then they answered him, saying, 'my lord, how did we see you hunger or thirst or sojourning or naked or sick or in a house of prisoners, and did not minister to ye?' Then he will answer them, saying, 'Confirmed [is] what I say to ye, that as you did not do one of these little ones, you did not do it to me. And these will go to a forfeiture of eternity, and the righteous to the eternal life.
