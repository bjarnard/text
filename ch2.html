Hebrew Matthew

Ch. 1

And afterwards, when Yeshua was born in Beit-Lechem, in a city of Judah in the days of Horodos the king, that sorcerers {seers in stars} came from the east to Jerusalem saying, “where [is] he that is born king of the Yehudim? For we have his his star in the east, and we have come {with thoughtful gifts} to worship him.” And when Horodos heard he was filled with rage {and he was confounded}, he and all Yerushalayim {and he gathered all the great-ones}, and he gathered all the chief priests, and the scribes of the nation, and he inquired of what place the Messiah would be born. And they said to him, “In Beit Lechem for thus is it said from the mouth of the prophet, ‘and thou Beith Lechem {Yehudah Ephratah, land of Yehudah, behold you are small among the thousands of Yehudah} say thou, ‘I will not be small among the thousands of Yehudah.’ From you comes forth to me he who is to be ruler of my people Israel [and his goings forth are of old, from the days of eternity: therefore he will give them until the the time that she who gives birth has labored, and the remainder of his brothers return unto the children of Israel: and he will stand and feed in the strength of Yehovah, in the majesty of the Name of Yehovah his Elohim, and he shall abide, for now he will be great unto the ends of the earth]. Then Horodos called the sorcerers {the diviners} in secret {and he inquired of them} and searched well of them the time at which the star appeared to them: And he sent them to Beit Lechem and he said, “go ye, and seek out {and ask ye} well concerning the boy, and when ye have found him, tell ye me so that I may also myself come to worship him. And when they heard the king, they went and lo, the star which they saw in the east went before them until it came and stood over and in front of the place where the boy {the child} [was]. And as they came towards the star they rejoiced with exceedingly great joy. And they went to the house and they found the boy and Miryam his mother with him {and they bowed down before him} and they fell upon the earth and they worshipped him {and they opened their sacks and brought forth gifts} and they opened their treasures and brought gifts near to him of gold, and frankincense, and myrrh. And it came to be that they were gripped with sleep and lo the Angel of Yehovah appeared to them saying, ‘keep yourselves from returning to Jerusalem to Horodos.’ And they returned to their own land going by a different way. And after they left, behold, [the] angel of Yehovah appeared to Yoseph in a dream saying, “Rise, take the boy and his mother and flee you to Mitsrayim, and you will be there and there you shall remain until I return to you {until I tell you} for Horodos seeks to put the boy to death {to murder the lad.} And he awoke and did as the Angel told him, and he bore up {took} the boy and his mother in the night. And he was there until the death of Horodos to fulfill what is spoken by Yehovah at the hand of the prophet, saying, “from Mitsrayim I have called my son.” Then Horodos saw that he was derided by the the sorcerers }then Horodos saw that they had beheld [all] {*that they mocked*}, and he was exceedingly evil inclined towards him} he was very angry {and it grieved his heart and he commanded} and he sent and put to death all the boys {the male children} that were in Beit Lechem and in all her borders from two years [old] and below, according to the timing he heard from the sorcerers {from the time that the diviners told him that the boy would be born}. Then was established what has been said by Yirmeyahu the prophet, Peace be upon him, saying, “a voice in Ramah [in a high place/on high/lifted up]: wailing, bitter weeping. Rachel, crying for her children with none to comfort her over her sons, for they are not.” And after Horodos died, behold the Angel of Yehovah appeared to Yoseph in a dream in Mitsrayim, saying “Rise, and bear up the boy and his mother, and go to the land of Israel for those who seek the child’s life are dead. And he rose and took the child and his mother and he came to the land the land of Israel. And when he heard that {Horqanos, whose name is Argilaus} reigned in Judah in his father Horodos’ place, he feared to go there, and he was warned in [his] sleep to go to the land of Galil {Gilgal}.
