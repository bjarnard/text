"The Kingdom of heaven is like a man, a master of the house, that went forth at the beginning of the morning to hire workers to labour [in] his vineyard. And when he had contracted with them at the rate of a zuz for the whole day, then he sent them to his vineyard. And he went forth at the third hour, and he saw others standing in [the] market unoccupied, and he said to them, 'go ye also to my vineyard, and what is right I will give ye.' And they went. Again he went forth at the sixth hour, and ninth, and did thus. And at exactly the eleventh hour, he went forth and he found others standing, and he said to them, 'why are ye standing here uselessly all day?' And they said to him, 'no man has hired us.' And he said to them, 'go ye also to my vineyard.' And when it came to be evening, the Lord of the vineyard said to his assistant, 'call the workers, and render to them a wage, and begin with the first up until the last.' And when they came who came in the eleventh hour, every man took his zuz. And the first came, and thought that they would take more than these, but they took every man a zuz. And when they had taken it, they went to the master of the house, and said, "these last-ones worked for one hour, and they are made equal to us that bore the burden of the day and heat." And he answered one of them, and he said, "My brother, I do no violence to you. Did you not contract with me for a zuz. Take that which [is] yours a go. It is my pleasure to give to this last-one, as well as unto you. Am I not allowed to do what I [find] pleasing in my eyes? Or is your eye is evil because I am good? Therefore the last will be first and the first [be] last. For many are called, but few are chosen. And Yeshua went up to Yerushalayim, and he took his twelve taught-ones in secret, and he said to them, "behold, we go up to Yerushalayim, and the Son of man will be delivered bound to the hand of the chief priests, and the scribes, and they shall bind him over to death, and they will deliver him bound to the Gentiles to be mocked, and scourged, and crucified, and the third day he shall rise." The the mother of the two sons of Zabdi drew near to him with her sons, and she bowed down, and she would ask [something] of him. And he said said to her, "What do you desire? And she spoke to him, saying, "That these my two sons may sit, one on your right, and one on your left, in your Kingdom." And Yeshua answered her, and he said, "ye do not know what ye ask. Are ye able to to drink the cup that I will drink, or to be immersed in the immersion that I will be immersed in? They can, "we are able." And he said to them, "indeed, my cup ye shall drink, and ye shall be immersed in the immersion that I will be immersed in, but to sit on my right hand or on my left hand, it is not for me to give to ye, but rather [is] for them who [it] is prepared of my Father." And the ten heard, and were angry at the two brothers. And Yeshua called them to him, saying, "ye know that the princes of the Gentiles rule over them, and their great ones have dominion among them. It shall not be so among ye, but rather, everyone who wants to great among ye, he will be your attendant. And he who want to be first, he shall be your servant. Likewise, so the Son of man has not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his being [as] a redemption-price for many." And when they had went forth from Yericho, huge crowds walked behind him. And behold, two blind people came forth, and were sitting on the road. And they heart that Yeshua was passing by, and they cried out, saying, "Adonai, show us favour. Son of David! And the crowd tried to make them give it a rest, saying, "be quiet!" And they cried out more, saying, "Adonai! Show us favour, son of David!" And Yeshua stood [still], and he called to them, saying, whatsoever would please you, this I will do for ye." And they said to him, "Lord, that our eyes be opened." And Yeshua showed them favour, and he touched their eyes, and immediately they saw, and followed after him.
