And Yeshua answered, and spooke again to them in parables, saying, "The Kingdom is like a man [who is] a king that organized a wedding for his son, and his sent his servants to invite called ones to the wedding...and they didn't want to come. Again he sent other servants, saying, saying, "say to my called ones, behold, I have prepared my covenant-banquet. My bull and swan are slaughtered. And all they who were prepared came to the feast. Yet these were negligent. And afterwards they left, one to his town, and one to his merchandise, and the remnant took hold of his servants, and killed them oppressively. And when the king heard, his anger was kindled, and he sent his army, and he destroyed these killers, and burnt their cities with fire. Then he said to his servant, "The marriage-feast is ready, and the called-ones were not worthy. And ye, go to the outgoings of the roads, and all them that ye find, call to the wedding-feast. And his servants went forth to the roads, and they gathered who they found, the bad and the good, and they filled the marriage-feast [with] people sitting at the party. And the King came to one of the people sitting and he saw there that the man was not clothed with a wedding garment. And he said to him, 'neighbor, how did you come hither, and how do you not have a wedding garment?' And he was silent. He the king said to his servants, 'bind his hands, and his feet, and send him to outter darkness. For there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth there, for many are called, and few are chosen." Then the Prushim left and counseled to take him away for this saying.
