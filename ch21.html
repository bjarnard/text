And when they drew near to Yerushalaim, and came to Beit-Phagei, to the moutain of the Olives, then Yeshua sent two taught-ones, and said to them, "go to the villa that is before ye, and right away ye shall find there a bound donkey, and a colt with her. Loose [them] and bring [them] near to me. And if a man says, "What?" to ye, say ye, "because Adonai has need of them." And right away he will let ye. And this is to establish what is said at the hand of the prophet, saying, "Speak to [the] daughter of Tsion, 'behold, your king comes to you meek, and riding upon a donkey, and upon a colt, a son of a she-ass." And the taught-ones went, and they did as Yeshua commanded them. And they brought near the she-ass and the colt, and they placed their garments upon them, and they had him ride from above. And most of the crowd stripped their garments off on[to] the road and cut [down] tree branches and spread them in the road. And the crowds that walked before him and after him called out, saying, "Please save the Son of David! Blessed [is he] that comes in the name Yehovah. Please save in [the] highest places!" And when he came to Yerushalaim, all the city made a noise, saying, "Who is this?" And the people said, "He is Yeshua the Prophet from Netseret, from Galil." And Yeshua came to the Temple of Elohim, and he cast out all sellers and buyers in the Temple, and the tables of the bankers, and the seats of those who sold the doves, he overturned. And he said to them, "It is written that my house shall be called a house of prayer, and ye make it a den of raiders." And the blind and lame drew near to him the Temple, and he healed them. And the chiefs of the priests and the scribes saw the miracles that he did, and the the young men calling out in the Temple, and saying, "Please save the Son of David!" and they were enraged, and they said to him, "Listen [to] what these are saying!" And Yeshua said to them, "Have ye not read, 'From the mouth of sucklings and nurslings you haved established strength.'" And he left them, and he went without to a city, to Beit Anyah, and he dwelt there, and he turned in [the] morning towards the city, and he hungered. And he saw a fig tree on the way, and he came to it, and he found not but leaves only within it. And he said to it, "fruit will never come forth from thee." And suddendly the fig tree withered. And the taught-ones were astonished, saying, "How suddenly it withered!" And Yeshua answered and said to them, truly I say to ye, if steadfastness be yours, and ye refuse to settle [for less], not only as the fig tree will ye do, but if ye say to this mountain, be lifted up and cast to sea, so it shall come to pass. And all that ye ask in prayer, and in steadfastness, ye shall recieve." And when he came into the the Temple, and when he was teaching, the chief priests, and elders of the nation, drew near to him, saying, "How are you able to do these [things], and who gave you this ability? And Yeshua answered, and he said to them, "I will ask ye also one saying, and if ye tell me it, I also will tell ye by what abilities I have done what I have done. The Immersion of Yochanan--was it rather from Heaven, or was it from men?" And they calculated amongst themselves, and they said, "If we say 'from Heaven,' he will say to us, 'why did ye not believe in him?' And if we say, 'from men,' we will fear the crowd." For in the eyes of all, Yochanan was as a prophet. And they said to Yeshua, "we don't know." And he said, "also as he [is] to ye, so I will I not say to ye by what ability I have don't these [things]. And what do ye behold: There was one man, and he had two sons. And the first drew near to him, and he said to him, "my son, go today to my vineyard, and work." And he answered, and said, "I will not do [so]." And his son returned afterwards, and went [to work]. And the second [son] drew near, and he said, "Also [go], son," and he answered and said, "I will go, my lord" and he didn't go. Who of these two did the will of the father?" They answered him, "the first." And Yeshua said to them, "Confirmed [is] what I say to ye, that master transgressors and prostitutes enter the kingdom of Elohim before ye. For Yochanan came to ye in The Righteous Way, and ye believed him not. But these master transgressors and prostitutes believed him. And ye see, but don't do repentance afterwards. Wherefore, to those faithful to him, hear another parable: There was a man who was a master of the house, and he planted a vineyard, removed him the stones, and dug a wine press. And he built a tower, and he handed it over to vintners to work it, and he went to sojourn [abroad], and when the time of fruits drew near, he sent his servant to the vintners, to take the fruit.
