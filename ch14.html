At that time Horodos, one of four princes, heard proclamation of Yeshua, and he said to his young men {his servants}, this is he [who is called] Yochanan the Immerser. He has risen from the dead, and therefore the powers work through him {behold, I believe these are the miracles done by Yochanan the Immerser}. And Horodos had Yochanan seized, and bound him, and put him in the prison house before Horodosit the wife of his brother [Herod II] because she said to him, "John is not allowed to be with you" {because he was reproving him, that he not take Ordisa to wife, since she was wife to his brother}. And he wanted to kill him, yet he feared the people, for he was a prophet in their eyes. And on the day of Horodos' birth, the daughter of Horodosit danced in the midst, and it was upright in the eyes of Horodos, and he he swore to her with an oath to give to her whatsoever she would ask of him. And she was instructed of her mother, and she said, "give me the head of Yochanan the Immerser here in a bowl." And it was grevious to the king. However, because of the oath he made and because of them that sat with him together at table, he gave commandment to give it to her, and he sent, and had the head of Yochanan cut off, who [is] in the jailer's house, so that his head could be carried in a bowl, and he could give it to the young girl. And they did so, and they gave [it] to the young girl, and she bore it to her mother. And his taught-ones drew near, and they removed his body, and they buried it. And they came, and declared it to the taught-ones of Yeshua. And when heard, they turned from there, in a boat, to a solitary place [in] the desert. And when the crowds heard, they went out from their cities, [going] after him on foot. And he went forth, and beheld many people, and he felt tender mercy for them, and he healed their sick. And afterwards, since there were so many, his taught-ones drew near to him, saying, "the place is desolate, and the [mealtime] hour already past, therefore, leave the crowds, that they might walk to the villages and buy food for themselves." And Yeshua said to them, "they don't have to go. You give them food!" An they answered him, "we don't have here but five loaves and two fish." And he lifted up his eyes to heaven, and he blessed and broke and gave the bread to his taught-ones, and his taught ones gave to the crowds, and they all ate, and were satisfied. And twelve baskets full of morsels were left over.
